About TennisIQ

Tennis data, re-imagined.

TennisIQ is a machine learning platform for tennis analytics. We combine historical match data, Glicko-2 ratings, and shot-level charting to produce the most accurate player models available outside the professional tour.

The Platform

What TennisIQ Does

TennisIQ turns publicly available tennis data into a comprehensive intelligence system. We process millions of match records, charted points, and historical ratings to produce FIFA-style player cards, head-to-head win predictions, and shot-pattern analytics.

Methodology

How the Models Work

The prediction pipeline goes through several stages to produce honest, calibrated win probabilities:

01

Glicko-2 Rating System

Surface-specific ratings (hard/clay/grass/all) computed from 1968 to present using Jeff Sackmann's ATP dataset. Handles rating uncertainty (RD) and volatility, with decay for inactive players.

02

Feature Engineering (152 Features)

Rolling serve/return stats, H2H history, surface-specific form, fatigue (days since last match, matches in 7/14 days), opponent-adjusted metrics, age curves, and rally-based features from charted data.

03

Stacked Ensemble

XGBoost and LightGBM base models trained on 90% of data (2010-2023). A logistic regression meta-learner stacks their outputs. Temporal hold-out test (2024) ensures zero leakage.

04

Player Card System

Eight attributes (Serve, Groundstroke, Volley, Footwork, Endurance, Durability, Clutch, Mental) are derived from match statistics and normalized to 0-100. Tier classification (Legendary/Gold/Silver/Bronze) is based on overall Glicko-2 percentile.

0.1807
Brier Score — Stacked Ensemble on 2024 Temporal Hold-Out

Lower is better. A Brier score of 0.25 equals random chance. Our 0.1807 represents a 27.7% improvement over random, comparable to leading betting market models.

Data Sources

Standing on the Shoulders of Open Data

TennisIQ is built entirely on publicly available datasets. We are deeply grateful to the contributors who make this possible.

Jeff Sackmann / Tennis Abstract

The definitive open-source ATP/WTA match dataset. 60+ years of match results, player metadata, rankings, and serve/return stats. Used for all rating computations and ML training.

Match Charting Project

Point-by-point shot data for 7,160+ professional matches. Enables serve direction breakdown, rally length profiles, and shot-pattern analytics for 980+ players.

Open-Meteo Historical Weather

Historical weather data (temperature, wind, humidity) for tournament venues used during model training. Included as neutral defaults during inference.

ATP Tour

Official ATP player headshots via the ATP Tour media server. Used for player profile display. All headshots remain property of the ATP Tour.
